This week I explored the design brief Blabla corps gave us. I started to think about what kind of community hub I can build. 

Here is the list of concept ideas I have:
1. Financial Literacy Community Hub for Women (most interested in this)

A space where women can share finance tips, resources, and personal experiences. The idea is to create a supportive and non-intimidating environment for people who are just starting their investing journey, where they can learn from peers and feel more confident engaging with finance.

2. Food Community (more aligned with general team interests)

A platform where users share food deals, discounts, or apps like EatClub and TooGoodToGo to help each other save money. It could also include recommendations and real-time finds. However, I feel like this space is already quite saturated, so it might be harder to make something that feels genuinely new or innovative.

3. Design Community (more general idea)

A hub for designers to share resources, tools, and insights to stay relevant in the industry. This comes from noticing how scattered and noisy design content is across different platforms, and how hard it is to find authentic, useful information. That said, I do feel like this concept is a bit more generic and less exciting compared to the others.
